**Underpinning Contract: In-Car Video Streaming Service Integration**

**1. Goal**  
The In-Car Video Streaming Service Integration aims to provide a seamless and luxurious in-car entertainment experience for users of luxury electric vehicles. This feature allows passengers to stream high-definition video content directly within the car's infotainment system, supporting multiple platforms such as Netflix, YouTube, and other video streaming services. It ensures smooth video playback with high-quality audio, providing an immersive experience while the vehicle is stationary or in motion.

**2. Preconditions**  
- The vehicle must have a compatible infotainment system with internet connectivity (either via Wi-Fi or mobile data).
- The user must have an active subscription to the respective video streaming services.
- The vehicle's system must be properly configured to support video streaming integration.
- The user must have logged into their streaming service account within the infotainment system.
- The video streaming feature is only available when the car is parked, or if the vehicle’s self-driving mode is enabled, ensuring safety during vehicle operation.

**3. Main Scenario**  
1. Alise enters the vehicle and starts the infotainment system.  
2. Alise navigates to the "In-Car Video Streaming" section via the touchscreen interface.  
3. Alise selects her preferred video streaming service (e.g., Netflix) and logs into her account if required.  
4. Alise browses through the content available and selects a movie or TV show to watch.  
5. The video begins to stream in high-definition quality on the infotainment display, and Alise enjoys the content with audio synchronized through the vehicle's sound system.

**4. Alternative Scenario**  
1. Bob enters the vehicle and starts the infotainment system.  
2. Bob navigates to the "In-Car Video Streaming" section, but the system shows a message indicating that there is no active internet connection.  
3. Bob checks the vehicle's Wi-Fi settings or mobile hotspot settings and attempts to reconnect.  
4. If the issue persists, Bob is prompted to contact customer support or try again later.  
5. Bob can also use offline content if previously downloaded, or choose to access alternative entertainment options within the vehicle.

**5. Postconditions**  
- After a successful interaction, Alise or Bob can enjoy a video streaming experience on the car's infotainment system, with the video playing smoothly and the audio coming through the car’s speakers.  
- The video streaming feature is now actively running, and the user can interact with it, such as pausing, skipping, or adjusting volume as needed.  
- The system will maintain the streaming session until the vehicle is powered off, the user logs out, or the connection is lost.
