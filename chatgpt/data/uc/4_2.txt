**Underpinning Contract: User Preferences Management for Infotainment System**

---

**1. Title**  
User Preferences Management for Infotainment System

---

**2. Goal**  
The User Preferences Management system for the Infotainment System allows users to personalize their infotainment experience in luxury electric cars. It enables the storage, retrieval, and adjustment of various settings that influence the user’s interaction with the system, including but not limited to audio preferences, seat positioning, climate control settings, and interface themes. The system adapts to each individual user, offering tailored configurations to enhance comfort, convenience, and user satisfaction. This functionality ensures that every interaction feels seamless, intuitive, and personalized.

---

**3. Preconditions**  
- The user has created a personal profile within the vehicle’s infotainment system.
- The infotainment system is powered on and operational.
- The vehicle's sensors and internal systems are functioning properly (e.g., climate control, seat positioning).
- The user has access to the infotainment system’s main interface (via touch screen or voice commands).
- The vehicle is in an environment where user preferences can be actively applied (e.g., driving, stationary with the engine on).

---

**4. Main Scenario**  
1. **User Action:** Alise enters the vehicle and selects her user profile from the infotainment system’s welcome screen.  
   **Sub-System Response:** The system loads Alise's personalized settings, including audio preferences, seat adjustments, and climate control.

2. **User Action:** Alise adjusts the volume and selects a specific music playlist from her favorites using voice commands.  
   **Sub-System Response:** The system immediately adjusts the volume and begins playing the selected playlist based on Alise’s preferences.

3. **User Action:** Alise requests to change the ambient lighting color via the touchscreen interface.  
   **Sub-System Response:** The system displays available lighting options and adjusts the lighting accordingly after Alise’s selection.

4. **User Action:** Alise sets the desired cabin temperature using the climate control menu.  
   **Sub-System Response:** The system adjusts the cabin temperature based on her preference, activating the necessary air conditioning or heating functions.

5. **User Action:** Alise saves her new preferences after making adjustments to the seat position and mirror angles.  
   **Sub-System Response:** The system saves the adjusted settings to her profile, ensuring that they will be applied automatically the next time Alise enters the vehicle.

---

**5. Alternative Scenario**  
1. **User Action:** Bob tries to access his user profile but the infotainment system displays an error indicating that his profile is unavailable due to a system issue.  
   **Sub-System Response:** The system offers an option to select a default user profile or create a new one. Bob can either choose the default settings or proceed with creating a new profile.

2. **User Action:** Bob attempts to adjust the climate control settings, but the system fails to respond.  
   **Sub-System Response:** A message appears notifying Bob that there is a temporary malfunction in the climate control system, offering an option to control the temperature manually via physical knobs or buttons.

3. **User Action:** Bob requests to play a specific playlist, but the system is unable to load it due to connectivity issues.  
   **Sub-System Response:** The system notifies Bob of the connectivity issue and provides alternative options, such as playing offline content or choosing another playlist from his collection.

---

**6. Postconditions**  
- After successful interaction, Alise's personalized preferences are saved and immediately applied to the system, ensuring a seamless experience for the next use.
- The system reflects any changes made to user settings, such as volume adjustments, climate preferences, or seat positioning, and ensures that these preferences are available during the next interaction without requiring additional setup.
- If any issues arise, the system provides clear feedback and guidance to help the user resolve or bypass the issue, maintaining a smooth and user-friendly experience.
